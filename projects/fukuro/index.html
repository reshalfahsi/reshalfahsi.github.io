    Fukurō: Wheeled Soccer Robot Platform of UGM based on RoboCup Middle Size League



    Fukurō is a wheeled soccer robot platform of UGM based on RoboCup Middle Size League. The robot has a dimension of 43 × 50 × 78 cm with 19.4 kg weight. The robot plays a ball with 62-64 cm diameter and 390-430 gram weight in the 7.2 × 4.8 m field. The platform itself could be divided as robot mechanical system, electronic system and software architecture.

    Mechanical System


    The robot mechanical system consists of five base tiers. At the first base, there are three 90° Swedish wheels. Each of the wheels connected to the planetary gear motor with 20 kgf.cm torque using chain mechanism. On the second base, there are a dribbler and a kicker mechanism. The dribbler consists of two motors that keeping the ball attached to the robot. The kicker consists of a plunger and a solenoid. The plunger is driven by solenoid to kick the ball. The robot uses a laptop PC as a main controller which placed on the third base. In order to detect the ball and another robot in the field, the robot is equipped with vision system. The vision system consists of a camera and a convex mirror. The camera is projected upwards on the fourth base and the convex mirror is stick to the bottom side of the fifth base. This configuration allows the robot to see 360° in the field.

    Electronic System


    The robot electronic system consists of main controller board, arduino board, power supply unit and motor driver.
